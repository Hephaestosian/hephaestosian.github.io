Emotion Cards Learning to name what you feel

A child who can name a feeling can handle it. Emotion Cards makes that naming into something to collect and play with — 47 cards to gather, and five ways to play with them.

Five Ways to Play

  • 🧭I just felt this — find your mood in four colored quadrants
  • 🐰Say it with your body — act it out, someone guesses
  • 📖Feelings in a story — read a scene, pick the feeling
  • 🔄Find the pair — match a feeling to its opposite
  • 🐻Mind detective — ask questions, work out how someone else felt

What's Inside

  • ● 47 emotion cards: Not bare words — whole phrases like "I feel proud" or "I want to share."
  • ● Five ways to play: Name your mood, act it out, read it in a story, match opposites, and guess someone else's.
  • ● Alone or together: Each game shows how many players it wants — some are for two.
  • ● No server, no account: Everything stays on the device.

Emotional Safety

Hard feelings are never wrapped up in a cheerful tone. Sadness, fear and conflict are acknowledged carefully first — and when something serious comes up, the app gently points the child toward a trusted grown-up.
